# BrightMind Growth + AdSense Safety Checklist

## Before Applying To AdSense

- Use a real custom domain, not only a localhost or temporary preview URL.
- Keep Home, Articles, Article Detail, About, Contact, Privacy Policy, Terms, Disclaimer, `robots.txt`, `sitemap.xml`, `feed.xml`, and `ads.txt` live.
- Replace any placeholder team, address, or claim with true information.
- Keep at least 20 useful articles live, but prioritize depth and originality over article count.
- Avoid copied content, rewritten content without value, empty pages, broken links, and auto-generated thin articles.
- Do not place fake ad boxes or ask users to click ads.

## Search Traffic Setup

- Add the site to Google Search Console.
- Submit `https://your-domain.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Check indexing errors weekly.
- Use article titles that match real student searches, such as "Newton's Laws of Motion Class 9 Examples".
- Add 3-5 internal links from every new article to related articles.
- Update old exam/current-affairs pages when dates change.

## Safe Ways To Get More Views

- Share article links in relevant study groups without spamming.
- Create short YouTube/Instagram study summaries and link back to full articles.
- Answer student questions on forums with helpful summaries and a link only when relevant.
- Publish consistent weekly articles in school, competitive, and GK categories.
- Build downloadable revision tables only from your own content.

## Avoid These AdSense Risks

- Do not buy traffic, use click exchange, autosurf, bots, or paid-to-click services.
- Do not ask friends or users to click ads.
- Do not use misleading ad labels or place ads where users expect navigation/download buttons.
- Do not publish adult, violent, hateful, illegal, copyrighted, or exam-cheating content.
- Do not make claims like guaranteed selection, marks, jobs, or income.

## After AdSense Approval

- Add only the ad code given inside your AdSense account.
- Keep ads clearly separate from navigation and article actions.
- Start with limited placements: one in-article ad and one sidebar/bottom ad.
- Monitor policy center and Search Console after every layout/content change.
